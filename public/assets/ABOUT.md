# Lingua Universalis: The Art of Creation

## Overview
**Lingua Universalis** is an international exhibition project featuring artists from Cuba, Venezuela, Argentina, Armenia, and Russia. It posits that the language of art is the most ancient, universal, and understandable form of communication on the planet.

## Mission & Manifesto
The project aims to demonstrate that art's capabilities extend far beyond commercial gain or utility. It is an instrument for exploring the human condition and our relationship with the Universe. In an age of global fragmentation, the "Art of Creation" (pure, independent art) serves as a foundation for mutual understanding and a bridge between diverse cultures.

---

## Project Philosophy

### 1. The Core Paradox
A bridge between the **prehistoric** (shamanic cave art) and the **meta-modern** (digital cinematic art). The aesthetic is simultaneously ancient and futuristic.

### 2. The "Non-Interface" Principle
The digital experience is designed as a **site of discovery**, not a standard website. It avoids generic UI patterns (rigid grids, top navbars) in favor of a journey-based navigation.

### 3. The Illumination of Truth
Knowledge is hidden in the dark. The **"Flashlight"** or "Spark" is the primary metaphor for consciousness. Information reveals itself only when the viewer provides focus (the "Light").

### 4. Organic Chaos
Nature does not work in 90-degree angles. The layout is designed to feel **"grown" or "eroded"** rather than programmed, utilizing asymmetry and organic transitions.

### 5. The Universal Thread
A single frequency connects the first handprint on a cave wall to a digital frame. Every design element is part of a singular, interconnected whole.

## The Creators (Constellation of Creators)

*   **Joslen Orsini (Venezuela/Russia):** Researcher of prehistoric art and universal human identity.
*   **Thomas Harutunyan (Armenia/Russia):** Master of realistic and abstract installation, focusing on cross-cultural synergy.
*   **Omar Godines (Cuba/Russia):** Explores the synthesis of ancient myths and modern plastic language.
*   **Andrey Vaganov (Russia):** Media Artist using AI as an independent observer to reveal hidden connections in reality.
*   **Petr Tsvetkov (Russia):** Modernist/Abstract artist seeking a foundation for peace through the universal language of art.
*   **Evgeny Globenko (Russia):** Calligrapher and abstract artist exploring the themes of "Silence" and the "Holy Grail."
